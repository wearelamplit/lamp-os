## Notes / findings carried in from the session
- **Cross-variant OTA loop (bug, logged in TODO.md):** jacko was endlessly offering
  its standard build to the old 1.1.0 snafu lamp — `considerPeerForOta` **fails open**
  when the peer's channel is empty (old lamps don't advertise it). Snafu correctly
  rejects; it just loops. Flashing snafu to the current tip (which advertises its
  variant) is expected to stop it — a good SERIAL confirmation if I get there.
- heap fragmentation is **fine on the tip** — clean-boot churn holds `largest` at
  ~40–43 KB (PASS). The earlier 14.8 KB "FAIL" was a stale pre-reboot sample, and it
  was a *dev-build* read — so OTA wasn't even sourcing at the time. OTA fragmentation
  is **not a known issue**: the sender allocates nothing per offer, and the receiver's
  ~1.2 KB chunk bitmap only exists on an accepted stream that reboots on apply (can't
  accumulate). Never measured against the heap; only worth a signed+`LAMP_DEBUG` check
  if a symptom ever appears.
